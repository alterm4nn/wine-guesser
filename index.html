      Red Wine Grape Varieties Reference Guide

      Learn about the 10 most popular red wine grape varieties

      Cabernet Sauvignon

      Origin: Bordeaux, France

      Body: Full-bodied

      Tannins: High

      Acidity: Medium to High

      Key Aromas: Blackcurrant, black cherry, cedar, tobacco, green bell pepper, mint

      Characteristics: Known as the "king of red wine grapes," produces age-worthy wines with firm tannins and complex flavors

      Merlot

      Origin: Bordeaux, France

      Body: Medium to Full-bodied

      Tannins: Medium

      Acidity: Medium

      Key Aromas: Plum, black cherry, chocolate, vanilla, clove, bay leaf

      Characteristics: Softer and more approachable than Cabernet Sauvignon, with velvety texture and fruit-forward profile

      Pinot Noir

      Origin: Burgundy, France

      Body: Light to Medium-bodied

      Tannins: Low to Medium

      Acidity: High

      Key Aromas: Strawberry, raspberry, cherry, mushroom, truffle, rose, wet leaves

      Characteristics: Elegant and delicate, often described as the most romantic and frustrating grape to grow

      Syrah (Shiraz)

      Origin: Rh√¥ne Valley, France

      Body: Full-bodied

      Tannins: Medium to High

      Acidity: Medium to High

      Key Aromas: Blackberry, blueberry, black pepper, smoke, grilled meat, olive, lavender

      Characteristics: Bold and spicy, expressing different characters in cool vs warm climates

      Malbec

      Origin: Cahors, France (now famous in Argentina)

      Body: Full-bodied

      Tannins: Medium to High

      Acidity: Medium

      Key Aromas: Blackberry, plum, black cherry, cocoa, leather, tobacco, violet

      Characteristics: Rich and jammy with inky dark color, Argentina's signature grape

      Zinfandel

      Origin: Croatia (famous in California, USA)

      Body: Medium to Full-bodied

      Tannins: Medium

      Acidity: Medium

      Key Aromas: Blackberry, raspberry, cherry, licorice, black pepper, tobacco

      Characteristics: Bold and fruity, often with higher alcohol content and spicy notes

      Sangiovese

      Origin: Tuscany, Italy

      Body: Medium to Full-bodied

      Tannins: High

      Acidity: High

      Key Aromas: Cherry, strawberry, tomato leaf, herbs, balsamic, leather, tea

      Characteristics: The heart of Chianti, known for bright acidity and savory notes

      Tempranillo

      Origin: Rioja, Spain

      Body: Medium to Full-bodied

      Tannins: Medium to High

      Acidity: Medium to High

      Key Aromas: Cherry, plum, tomato, leather, vanilla, dill, tobacco

      Characteristics: Spain's noble grape, responds well to oak aging

      Grenache (Garnacha)

      Origin: Spain (famous in Southern Rh√¥ne, France)

      Body: Medium to Full-bodied

      Tannins: Low to Medium

      Acidity: Medium

      Key Aromas: Strawberry, raspberry, cherry, white pepper, herbs, orange peel

      Characteristics: Fruity and spicy, often blended but excellent on its own

      Nebbiolo

      Origin: Piedmont, Italy

      Body: Full-bodied

      Tannins: Very High

      Acidity: Very High

      Key Aromas: Rose, tar, cherry, truffle, leather, anise, tobacco, dried herbs

      Characteristics: Creates Barolo and Barbaresco, powerful wines with incredible aging potential
